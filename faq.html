Frequently Asked Questions

Because What You Can’t See Can Cost You

← Back to Home

  • What exactly does SkySure Inspections do?

    We use drones to capture high-resolution images and video of your roof, gutters, chimneys, and property from above. This helps spot issues early, document storm damage, and provide reports you can use with roofers, insurance companies, or real estate professionals.

  • How is this different from a traditional roof inspection?

    Traditional inspections require someone climbing onto your roof. Our method is faster, safer, and more thorough, with detailed aerial imagery that can reveal issues not easily seen on foot.

  • Are you licensed to fly drones?

    Yes. We are FAA Part 107 Certified, meeting all federal requirements to fly drones commercially and safely in residential areas.

  • How long does an inspection take?

    Onsite time is typically 30–45 minutes, and you’ll receive your completed report within 24 hours.

  • How often should I get my roof inspected?

    We recommend once a year for preventative maintenance, plus after major storms to catch potential damage early.

  • What kind of problems can a drone inspection detect?

    We commonly spot missing shingles, lifted shingles, cracked flashing, clogged gutters, ponding water, hail impact, and early signs of leaks.

  • Will this inspection damage my roof?

    No. Since we never have to walk on your roof, there’s zero risk of damage from ladders, tools, or foot traffic.

  • Can I use the report for an insurance claim?

    Yes. Our detailed photo reports are designed to be insurance-ready and can speed up claims by providing third-party evidence of storm damage.

  • Do realtors use this service?

    Absolutely. Realtors rely on our reports for pre-listing checks, buyer peace of mind, and negotiation leverage during a transaction.

  • Will roofers accept your findings?

    Yes. Many roofers prefer working with us because it saves them time and gives them a clear scope of work before they ever step on the roof.

  • How much does an inspection cost?

    Pricing depends on the property size and level of detail, but most inspections are $150–$300—far less than the cost of roof repairs or a failed claim.

  • How do I schedule an inspection?

    Schedule through our website form or by calling us. We confirm the appointment, handle the flight, and deliver your report digitally.

  • What do I get with my inspection?

    Aerial photos/video, annotated images highlighting issues, a written summary report (PDF), and optional referrals to trusted roofing professionals.

  • Can drones fly in bad weather?

    We avoid flying in rain, strong winds, or storms. If weather is an issue, we reschedule at no cost.

  • What if my home is near trees or power lines?

    Our drones have obstacle avoidance, and our pilots plan each flight to ensure safe operation.

  • Will neighbors be concerned about privacy?

    We only capture images of the property being inspected and follow FAA privacy and safety guidelines.

  • Do you offer annual service plans?

    Yes. Our Annual Roof Check Plan includes a scheduled yearly inspection plus storm-response inspections at discounted rates.

  • Can I be present during the inspection?

    Yes, though it’s not required—you’ll receive everything digitally even if you’re not home.

  • Do you only inspect roofs?

    Roofs are our specialty, but we also perform property flyovers, gutter checks, and visual inspections for insurance documentation.
